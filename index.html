I am a postdoctoral associate at MIT Linguistics and will be an associate professor at UCL Linguistics starting in fall 2024. I hold a PhD in Linguistics from the University of Chicago and was previously a Mellon Postdoctoral Fellow at Stanford University. I am primarily interested in syntax and its interfaces. Most of my research focuses on Malayo-Polynesian languages and indigenous languages of the Caucasus.

Research interests: argument alignment, polysynthesis, ergativity, fieldwork methodologies, case and licensing, syntactic locality, linguistic typology.

Current projects

  • Syntactic ergativity and the Ergative Extraction Constraint (PSST 2022; LSA 2023; UCL 2023; submitted ms.)
  • Interactions between case, nominal licensing, and agreement (UMass 2023; GLOW 2023)
  • Locality, phasehood, and the morphology-syntax interface (Leipzig 2023; chapter ms.)
  • Locality and antilocality (LASER 2024; submitted ms.)
  • Number neutrality and structure of DPs in West Circassian (with Nikita Bezrukov) (LASER 2024)
  • Wh-agreement and phi-features (CLS proceedings in press)
  • Agents and argument structure in passives (with Satyawidya Wulansari) (APLL 2024)
  • Thematic roles, ergativity, and inherent case
  • Applicatives and argument licensing in Indonesian (with Satyawidya Wulansari)
